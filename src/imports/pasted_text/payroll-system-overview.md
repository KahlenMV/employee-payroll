An employee payroll website/system usually contains several core modules so a company can manage employees, compute salaries, track attendance, handle deductions, and generate reports. Below are the most common and essential components.

1. Employee Management

This section stores and manages employee information.

Typical features:

Employee profile (name, ID, position, department)

Employment status (regular, contractual, probationary)

Salary information (basic pay, hourly rate)

Tax identification numbers

Government contributions

Bank account details for salary transfer

Employee documents (contracts, IDs)

2. Attendance and Time Tracking

Payroll depends heavily on attendance data.

Features:

Time-in and time-out logging

Leave tracking (sick leave, vacation leave, etc.)

Absences and tardiness monitoring

Overtime recording

Shift scheduling

3. Payroll Processing

This is the core function of the website.

Functions include:

Automatic salary calculation

Overtime pay computation

Holiday pay calculation

Leave deductions

Bonuses and incentives

Payroll period management (weekly, semi-monthly, monthly)

4. Deductions and Contributions

Payroll systems must manage deductions.

Common deductions:

Tax withholding

Government contributions:

SSS

PhilHealth

Pag-IBIG

Loans

Cash advances

Other company deductions

5. Payslip Generation

Employees should be able to view their payslips.

Features:

Payslip preview

Downloadable payslip (PDF)

Breakdown of earnings and deductions

Payroll history

6. Reports and Analytics

Managers and HR need financial summaries.

Typical reports:

Payroll summary report

Employee earnings report

Deduction reports

Government contribution reports

Department payroll cost

Year-end payroll summary

7. User Roles and Access Control

Different users need different access.

Common roles:

Admin

HR Manager

Payroll Officer

Employee (self-service portal)

This ensures security and proper data access.

8. Employee Self-Service Portal

Many modern payroll systems include this.

Employees can:

View payslips

Update personal information

Request leave

Track attendance

Download tax documents

9. Security and Compliance

Payroll systems handle sensitive data.

Important elements:

Secure login system

Data encryption

Audit logs

Backup and recovery

Compliance with labor and tax laws

10. System Integrations (Optional but Useful)

Advanced payroll websites integrate with:

Accounting systems

HR management systems

Biometric attendance devices

Banking systems for salary transfers
